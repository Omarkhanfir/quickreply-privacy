QuickReply — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-26

Who we are

QuickReply is an independently developed Android app that lets you reply to messages hands-free using your phone's volume buttons.

Contact: omarkhanfir93@gmail.com

What the app does with your notifications

QuickReply requests the Notification Access permission solely to detect incoming messages and send back a pre-written reply that you created inside the app.

Notification content — message text, sender names, images — is never read, stored, logged, or transmitted anywhere. All processing happens entirely on your device and stays there.

The app reads just enough of a notification to know that a repliable message arrived and which conversation to reply to. It then fires your own saved reply text using Android's standard RemoteInput API — the same mechanism a Bluetooth headset uses.

We do not sell, share, or have any access to your messages or contacts.

Data stored on your device

The only data QuickReply stores is what you enter yourself:

DataWhere it livesPurpose
Gesture patterns you createLocal app databaseTrigger the matching reply
Reply messages you writeLocal app databaseSent as your auto-reply
Schedules and timer settingsLocal app databaseControl when auto-reply is active
App preference (enabled/disabled)Local app storageRemember your on/off toggle

None of this data ever leaves your device.

Advertising — Google AdMob

QuickReply displays ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect and use your device's Advertising ID to show relevant ads. This is handled entirely by the AdMob SDK; QuickReply does not read, store, or transmit your Advertising ID itself.

For details on how Google uses this data, see Google's Privacy Policy.

You can reset or opt out of ad personalisation at any time in Android Settings → Google → Ads.

Permissions used

PermissionWhy it is needed
Notification AccessDetect repliable messages and send your pre-set replies
Foreground ServiceKeep gesture detection running while the screen is off
Wake LockHold the CPU awake briefly (~10 s) during the gesture window
Receive Boot CompletedRestart the service automatically after a reboot
Post NotificationsShow the persistent service status notification
Modify Audio SettingsTemporarily adjust ring volume for reliable gesture detection, then restore it
Request Ignore Battery OptimizationsAsk to be exempt from aggressive battery management so the service is not killed

No permission is used for data collection.

No accounts, analytics, or backend

QuickReply has no user accounts, no sign-in, no analytics SDK, no crash reporting service, and no backend server. The only third-party code present is the Google AdMob SDK for displaying ads.

Children

QuickReply is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from children.
